## URGENT WARNING — CIVIL SUIT LIMITATION

### THE CLOCK MAY HAVE RUN OUT ON THE CIVIL SUIT

Under the **Bangladesh Limitation Act 1908**, a civil money suit against Alesha Mart
must be filed **within 3 years** of the cause of action.

| Calculation | Date |
|-------------|------|
| Cause of action (failed delivery / refusal to refund) | ~July 2021 |
| Standard 3-year limit | **July 2024 — MAY HAVE EXPIRED** |

**DO NOT PANIC — three arguments exist to save the claim:**

1. **Section 19 (Acknowledgment):** The chairman's June 2022 public pledge to refund all
   customers by June 30, 2022 is a written acknowledgment of liability. This creates
   a new cause of action from June 30, 2022 (when he defaulted on that promise).
   This extends the limitation to **June 30, 2025** — which has also just passed.

2. **Section 18 (Fraud Concealment):** Where fraud kept SM Nabil from knowing his full
   legal rights, the clock runs only from when the fraud became fully known.
   Argue that SM Nabil reasonably believed the government refund process would pay
   him — only recently becoming clear it has failed.

3. **File Immediately:** Even if the court raises limitation, it must hear the argument.
   A lawyer can file today with Sections 18-19 arguments. Delay of even weeks makes
   this argument weaker.

**ACTION: Retain a lawyer today and file a civil suit this week.**

## WRIT OF MANDAMUS — NOT TIME-BARRED (CONTINUING WRONG)

The writ petition is the strongest and safest route. It is NOT affected by the
Limitation Act. Courts apply "laches" (unreasonable delay) but:

> *"Delay by the authorities may constitute a cause of action in itself, especially
> in a case of a live and continuing cause of action or in the event of failure to
> perform a mandatory statutory duty."* — Bangladesh High Court Division

Every day the CDCC fails to pay SM Nabil is a new wrong. The cause of action is
**continuous**. File the writ now.

## CRITICAL: DNCRP CANNOT HEAR SM NABIL'S CASE

**Section 60, Consumer Rights Protection Act 2009:**
DNCRP's jurisdiction is capped at claims **not exceeding BDT 200,000**.
SM Nabil's claim is BDT 500,620 — **2.5× the DNCRP limit**.

DNCRP was **never the right forum**. The correct forum is:
- **High Court Division** (Article 102 writ) — for government inaction
- **District Civil Court** — for money decree against Alesha Mart

Going to DNCRP would result in rejection on jurisdictional grounds. However,
that rejection letter is itself useful evidence of government failure for the writ.

## CONSTITUTIONAL TORT — GETTING PAID BY THE GOVERNMENT

**CCB Foundation v. Government of Bangladesh** (the "Jihad case"):
The High Court Division — confirmed by the Appellate Division — held:
1. The state is NOT immune from liability for its agencies' negligence
2. Courts CAN award **monetary compensation** under Article 102(1)
3. BDT 20 lakh (2,000,000) awarded for a fundamental rights violation

**SM Nabil's argument:** The state seized Alesha Mart assets and announced a refund
process. Taking custody of the recovery mechanism creates a duty to victims. Five years
of inaction is actionable negligence. The High Court can order the **government itself
to pay compensation** for the Article 42 (right to property) violation.
