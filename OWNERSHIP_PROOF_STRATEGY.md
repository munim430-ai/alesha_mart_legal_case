## THE LEGAL THEORY — THREE ANGLES

### Angle 1: Resulting Trust (Strongest)
Under Bangladesh equity law (following English trust law, applicable in
Bangladesh via the Specific Relief Act and equitable jurisdiction of courts):

> *Where A provides the money and B places a transaction in B's own name,
> B holds the transaction proceeds on resulting trust for A.*

Hasibul's mother provided the money. SM Nabil placed the transactions.
Therefore SM Nabil holds the claim (and any refund) on resulting trust
for Hasibul's family. This is automatic in equity — no formal trust deed needed.

**Case law:** The Bangladesh High Court Division regularly applies resulting trust
principles. The money's origin is the decisive factor, not whose name is on
the invoice.

### Angle 2: Agency Relationship
SM Nabil acted as the **agent** of Hasibul's mother. He received cash from
the principal (the mother) and used it on her instruction to purchase goods.
Under the Contract Act 1872 (Sections 182–238 on Agency):

- The principal (mother) is the true claimant
- The agent (SM Nabil) acted on behalf of the principal
- All rights arising from the agent's act vest in the principal

Any refund from Alesha Mart / CDCC legally belongs to the principal (mother),
not the agent (SM Nabil).

### Angle 3: Unjust Enrichment (Backstop)
If SM Nabil were to receive a refund and keep it, he would be unjustly
enriched at the expense of Hasibul's family. Bangladesh courts will order
restitution to prevent unjust enrichment.

## WHAT "PROOF" THE COURT WILL ACCEPT

Bangladesh civil courts (and the High Court Division in writ proceedings) apply
the civil standard of proof: **balance of probabilities** (not beyond reasonable doubt).

You do NOT need to prove the cash transfer with absolute certainty.
You need to show it is MORE LIKELY THAN NOT that:
1. The money came from the father's pension ✓ (bank statement proves this)
2. The mother gave it to SM Nabil ✓ (SM Nabil's affidavit proves this)
3. SM Nabil placed the orders with that money ✓ (invoices prove this)
4. The family is the true owner ✓ (all of the above, plus relationship evidence)

**On balance of probabilities, this chain is easy to establish.**
